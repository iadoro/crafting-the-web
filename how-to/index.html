1. Child's Pose, Wide Knee (Balasana)

Start seated in a kneeling position with shins flat on the ground. Keep the knees slightly wider than the torso. Walk hands forward so that arms fully lengthen and forehead is rested on the ground.

2. Thread the Needle (Urdhva Mukha Pasasana)

Lift head up from Child's Pose. Bring right hand from off the mat and into the gap made by the left arm and left leg. Lay the right arm flat on the ground and lean down. Repeat on other side.

3. Supported Pigeon Pose (Salamba Kapotasana)

Start in kneeling position. Extend one leg back and stablize arms in front. Push into back while keeping legs pressed against floor. Repeat on other side.

4. Down Dog (Adho Mukha Shvanasana)

Start in kneeling position. Lift knees so that they are unbent and stabilize arms in front. Push back into legs to create an upside down "V" shaped silhouette.

5. Lazy Warrior III (Lazy Virabhadrasana III)

From Down Dog, lift one leg so that it is parallel to the floor. Make sure to keep arms stabilized. Lift higher if hips allow. Alternate between flexing and keeping foot at a point. Repeat on other side.

6. Reverse Arm Hold (Pashchima Namaskarasana)

Can do this stretch either while standing or kneeling. Interlock fingers from both hands behind the back and pull down and outwards.
